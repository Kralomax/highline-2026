Trail Advisory

Tonto National Forest advises avoiding camping near drainages recently impacted by the Washington Fire along Passage 26. This includes the 2-mile stretch between Chase Creek (AZT mile 477.1) and the two drainages to the east. We'll plan our route accordingly.

A Trail with Stories

The Highline Trail was established in 1870, originally used by pioneers to travel between homesteads and for children to attend school in Pine. It's one of Arizona's oldest and most historic trails.

Famous western author Zane Grey used this trail while hunting with local guide Babe Haught. The landscape inspired many of his novels, and you can still feel the wild west spirit as you walk these paths.

The trail passes near the site of the Battle of Big Dry Wash (1882), the last major conflict between Apache warriors and U.S. military forces in Arizona.

Designated a National Recreation Trail in 1979, the Highline has been restored and improved through collaborative efforts between the Arizona Trail Association and Tonto National Forest.

üí° Family Reunion Planning Tip

Here's how we recommend organizing the trip if you have both hikers and non-hikers:

  • Day 1: Everyone meets at basecamp (cabin/lodge in Pine area). Hikers get dropped off at Pine Trailhead.
  • Days 1-2: Non-hikers explore Tonto Natural Bridge, shop in Pine/Strawberry, relax at the cabin.
  • Day 3: Non-hikers drive to Washington Park Trailhead to pick up hikers. Celebration dinner in Payson!
  • Bonus: Book Goodfellow Lodge or Cabins on Strawberry Hill for a proper reunion basecamp where everyone can gather.
