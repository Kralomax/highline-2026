üèîÔ∏è Trail Overview

Blue Ridge (Passage 27) is the PERFECT family backpacking trip - shorter distance, moderate difficulty, and you start ON TOP of the Mogollon Rim!

‚ú® Why Your Family Will Love This Trail

START ON TOP! Begin at 7,600' on the Mogollon Rim - skip the brutal climb. You start where the Highline Trail finishes!

SHORTEST OPTION: Only 15.4 miles over 2-3 days. Perfect for mixed experience levels and "old legs."

BEST WATER: East Clear Creek is cold, clear, reliable, and perfect for an afternoon fishing session!

FLEXIBLE: Do 2 days (faster) or 3 days (relaxed). Your choice!

MODERATE DIFFICULTY: Only 1,397' total elevation gain. Gentle ups and downs, no killer climbs.

üìç Trail Details

Location: General Springs (Washington Park) to Blue Ridge Ranger Station / Highway 87

Distance: 15.4 miles point-to-point

Elevation Range: 7,000' - 7,600' (high ponderosa pine country)

Best Season: April-October (September is PERFECT - warm days, cool nights)

Water Sources: Stock pond, East Clear Creek (‚≠ê RELIABLE), Miller Canyon Creek, Blue Ridge Reservoir (variable)

Difficulty: Moderate (AllTrails rates it challenging, but that's relative to day-hikers)

Permits: None required for backpacking! (Coconino National Forest)

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Considerations

FR-300 Access: 12 miles of DIRT ROAD to General Springs Cabin. Doable in 2WD but rough. Check road conditions before departure!

Creek Crossing: East Clear Creek requires wet feet - bring sandals or embrace soggy boots.

Alternative Start: If FR-300 is too rough, start from Highway 87 and hike southbound instead.

September Weather: Expect 70-80¬∞F days, 40-50¬∞F nights. Afternoon thunderstorms possible.

üëü Wet Feet Strategy

Creek crossing is unavoidable. Options: (1) Bring camp sandals/Crocs for crossing, (2) Take boots off and wade barefoot, (3) Just walk through in boots and let them dry. We recommend option 1 or 2 for comfort!

‚ö° 2-Day Option (Faster Pace)

üé£ East Clear Creek Fishing Guide

Wild trout fishing in one of Arizona's most beautiful high-country streams!

üêü What You'll Catch

Rainbow Trout: Self-sustaining wild population, 8-12" average, some up to 14-16"

Brown Trout: Stream-bred wild browns, 10-14" average, trophy fish up to 18"+

Brook Trout: Less common, 8-10" average, beautiful coloration

Best Feature: WILD FISH! No stocking - these are stream-bred native trout

üéØ Fishing Strategy

Best Times

Morning: Early morning (6-9 AM) - fish are active after cool night

Evening: Late afternoon/evening (4-7 PM) - prime feeding time

Overcast Days: Fish all day! Clouds bring out the trout

After Rain: Wait 1 hour for water to clear, then excellent fishing

üí° Pro Tips

Tenkara Advantage: For backpacking, a Tenkara rod (Japanese fixed-line fly fishing) is PERFECT. Lightweight, simple, no reel needed. Perfect for small streams!

Fly Selection: East Clear Creek has excellent insect hatches. Small mayflies and caddisflies in September. Match the hatch!

Stealthy Approach: Wild trout are spooky. Approach from downstream, keep low profile, don't cast shadows on water.

Catch & Release: These are wild fish - release them gently to preserve the fishery for future anglers!

üí° Weight-Saving Tips

The Big Three: Your backpack, tent, and sleeping bag/pad are ~60% of your base weight. Upgrade these for biggest impact!

Share Gear: One tent for 2 people, split cook system weight, share first aid kit.

Wear Your Heavy Stuff: Boots and rain jacket don't count toward "base weight" if worn while hiking.

Multi-Use Items: Trekking poles = tent poles, bandana = towel/washcloth/sun protection.

üèÉ 12-Week Training Plan

Prepare your body for Blue Ridge! This gentle progressive plan gets you ready for 2-3 days of backpacking.

üéØ Training Goals for Blue Ridge

Endurance: Comfortable hiking 5-7 miles/day with a pack

Pack Weight: Able to carry 20-25 lbs comfortably

Elevation: Handle 400-700' elevation gain per day

Terrain: Navigate rocky trails, creek crossings, gentle climbs

"Old Legs" Factor: Blue Ridge is MODERATE - perfect for this training level!

üìÖ Weekly Schedule

üîπ Weeks 1-2: Base Building

  • 3x per week: Walk 2-3 miles on flat terrain, no pack
  • 1x per week: 30-minute wall sits progression (start 30 seconds, add 15 sec/week)
  • Daily: Gentle stretching (hamstrings, calves, hip flexors)
  • Goal: Build walking habit, get joints moving

üîπ Weeks 3-4: Add Hills

  • 3x per week: Walk 3-4 miles with GENTLE hills/inclines
  • 1x per week: Wall sits 45-60 seconds (3 sets)
  • 1x per week: Stair walking 10-15 minutes (up AND down practice)
  • Add: Single-leg balance practice (30 sec each leg, 3x/day)
  • Goal: Introduce elevation change, ankle stability

üîπ Weeks 5-6: Add Pack Weight

  • 3x per week: Walk 4-5 miles with 10 lb pack (books, water bottles)
  • 1x per week: Wall sits 60-75 seconds (3 sets)
  • 1x per week: Hill/stair workout with pack (20 min)
  • Add: Downhill practice - eccentric squats, controlled descents
  • Goal: Get used to carrying weight, strengthen knees for downhills

üîπ Weeks 7-8: Increase Pack Weight

  • 3x per week: Walk 5-6 miles with 15 lb pack
  • 1x per week: Wall sits 90 seconds (3 sets)
  • 1x per week: 6-mile hike on actual trail (if possible) with 15 lb pack
  • Add: Rock hopping practice (improve ankle stability)
  • Goal: Build endurance with realistic pack weight

üîπ Weeks 9-10: Peak Training

  • 2x per week: Walk 6-7 miles with 18-20 lb pack
  • 1x per week: Wall sits 2 minutes (2-3 sets)
  • 1x per week: Long practice hike 8-10 miles with 20 lb pack
  • Add: Pack all your actual gear, test everything!
  • Goal: Peak fitness, gear shakedown

üîπ Weeks 11-12: Taper & Prep

  • Week 11: Reduce to 4-5 miles, 2x with light pack (recovery week)
  • Week 12: Easy 3-4 mile walks, light stretching only
  • Final prep: Pack shakedown, gear test, blister prevention
  • Goal: Arrive fresh and ready!

üéØ Key Exercises Explained

Wall Sits: Back against wall, slide down to 90¬∞ knee bend, hold. Builds quad endurance for long days.

Eccentric Squats: Slow 5-second descent into squat. Strengthens muscles for downhills (Blue Ridge Day 3!).

Single-Leg Balance: Stand on one leg, 30 seconds. Improves ankle stability for rocky terrain.

Stair Step-Downs: Step down slowly from stair, control descent. Protects knees on downhills.

üíä Nutrition & Recovery

Hydration: Drink 64+ oz water daily. Practice electrolyte drinks (Liquid IV, LMNT) on training hikes.

Protein: 0.7-1g per lb body weight to support muscle recovery.

Rest Days: Take 1-2 rest days per week. Your body needs recovery!

Stretching: Focus on hamstrings, calves, hip flexors, IT band. 10 min daily.

Sleep: 7-9 hours per night. Sleep is when adaptation happens!

üöó Logistics & Permits

Everything you need to know about getting there, parking, shuttles, and permits.

üÖøÔ∏è Trailhead Access & Parking

Southern Trailhead: General Springs Cabin

GPS: 34.4733¬∞ N, 111.3397¬∞ W

From Payson:

1. Take AZ-87 north toward Strawberry/Pine (26 miles)

2. Turn RIGHT onto FR-300 (Rim Road) - sign says "General Springs"

3. Drive 12 miles on DIRT/GRAVEL road (rough but doable in 2WD)

4. Turn LEFT at Battle of Big Dry Wash historical marker

5. Follow powerline road 0.3 miles to General Springs Cabin

Parking: Small lot at cabin (fits 6-8 cars). Overflow parking along road.

Drive Time from Phoenix: ~2.5 hours

‚ö†Ô∏è FR-300 Road Conditions

Passability: Usually passable in 2WD sedan in dry weather. High clearance vehicle PREFERRED.

Seasonal Closures: FR-300 closes in winter (snow/ice). Usually open April-November.

CHECK BEFORE YOU GO: Call Mogollon Rim Ranger District: (928) 477-2255

Alternative: Start from Highway 87 trailhead and hike southbound if FR-300 is rough.

Northern Trailhead: Highway 87 / Blue Ridge

GPS: 34.6075¬∞ N, 111.2006¬∞ W

Location: Junction of AZ-87 and FR-138, ~0.8 miles west of Blue Ridge Ranger Station

From Payson: Drive AZ-87 northeast for 19.5 miles

Parking: Trailhead parking area on FR-138 (100 yards south of highway)

Access: PAVED highway access - much easier than FR-300!

üöê Shuttle Options

Option 1: Two-Vehicle Shuttle

  • Park 1 car at Northern Trailhead (Highway 87)
  • Drive everyone in 2nd car to Southern Trailhead (General Springs)
  • Hike north, pick up car at Highway 87
  • Return to General Springs to retrieve 2nd vehicle
  • Shuttle Drive Time: ~45 minutes one-way via FR-300

Option 2: Paid Shuttle Service

  • Contact local Payson/Pine area shuttle services
  • Typical cost: $75-150 for trailhead shuttle
  • Book 2-3 weeks in advance (limited services)
  • Try: Payson area taxi services or ask at Pine/Strawberry lodges

Option 3: Out-and-Back

  • Start at Highway 87 (north), hike south to East Clear Creek, return
  • ~13 miles round trip to creek and back
  • Skip Day 1, focus on Day 2 fishing + Day 3 scenery
  • No shuttle needed!

üìú Permits & Regulations

‚úÖ Good News: NO PERMITS REQUIRED!

Backpacking: No permit needed for overnight camping in Coconino National Forest

Camping: Camp anywhere along trail, practice Leave No Trace

Fires: Check current fire restrictions (often banned May-October)

Groups: No group size limit, but keep groups small to minimize impact

‚ö†Ô∏è Forest Service Regulations

Camping: Camp 200+ feet from water sources and trails when possible

Human Waste: Cat holes 6-8 inches deep, 200+ feet from water. Pack out TP!

Food Storage: Hang food or use Ursack. Black bears present but not common.

Dogs: Allowed on-leash. Must be under control at all times.

Fishing License: REQUIRED if fishing East Clear Creek (see fishing section)

üöÅ Emergency Evacuation

If Serious Injury/Illness Occurs

1. Stabilize: Stop bleeding, treat for shock, keep patient warm

2. Call 911: If you have cell service, call immediately with GPS coordinates

3. Send Runner: If no cell service, send fastest hiker to trailhead to call 911

4. Mark Location: Note GPS coordinates, describe landmarks for rescue

5. Stay Put: Unless unsafe, stay with injured person until help arrives

Helicopter Landing: Blue Ridge area has open meadows suitable for helicopter rescue if needed
